## Why Your Smart Home Needs a Professional Network

The number one complaint from smart home owners is unreliable WiFi — devices dropping offline, slow response times, and dead zones. Here's why consumer-grade networking falls short:

**Device count.** A modern smart home can have 50-100+ WiFi-connected devices. Consumer routers start struggling at 20-30 devices. Professional access points handle 100+ devices without breaking a sweat.

**Coverage.** Minneapolis homes vary from 1,200 sq ft bungalows to 6,000+ sq ft homes. A single router can't cover a large home reliably, especially with plaster walls common in older Minneapolis neighborhoods.

**Interference.** Dense Minneapolis neighborhoods (especially Uptown, Northeast, and Loring Park condos) have massive WiFi congestion. Professional systems use intelligent channel management and band steering to maintain performance.

**Reliability.** Smart locks, security cameras, and climate systems need 24/7 connectivity. Consumer mesh systems are "good enough" for streaming but aren't reliable enough for security and automation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I really need professional WiFi, or is my Eero/Google Wifi good enough?**

Consumer mesh systems like Eero and Google Wifi work fine for 2-3 people streaming and browsing. But if you have more than 20 smart devices, work from home on video calls, or plan to add [security cameras](/services/smart-home-security) and automation, you'll quickly outgrow consumer mesh. Professional systems cost more upfront but eliminate the frustration of unreliable smart devices.

**Can you run Ethernet in an existing Minneapolis home?**

Yes. Our installers use techniques like fishing cables through walls, running through unfinished basements (very common in Minneapolis), and using existing conduit paths. Most Minneapolis homes can have 6-12 Ethernet drops installed in a single day without major construction.

**What internet speed do I need for a smart home?**

Most smart home devices use very little bandwidth individually, but the total adds up. We recommend at least 200 Mbps for homes with security cameras and 20+ devices. For heavy streaming households with 4K cameras, 500 Mbps+ is ideal. Your ISP service (Xfinity, CenturyLink Fiber, or US Internet in Minneapolis) matters less than your internal network quality.

**How often does network equipment need to be replaced?**

Professional-grade networking equipment typically lasts 7-10 years before needing replacement. Compare this to consumer routers that often need replacement every 3-4 years. The higher upfront cost of professional equipment is offset by longer lifespan and better performance.
