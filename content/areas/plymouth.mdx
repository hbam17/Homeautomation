## Smart Home Challenges Unique to Plymouth

Plymouth's housing stock spans five decades. Homes near Plymouth Creek Park and the Bass Lake area, built primarily in the 1970s and 1980s, often have 100-amp electrical panels, limited low-voltage wiring, and construction techniques (plaster or early drywall over insulation) that can attenuate Wi-Fi signals. Upgrading these homes requires careful planning around existing infrastructure.

The city's generous lot sizes — especially in the Fernbrook and Wayzata West areas — create networking challenges. Detached garages, large yards, and multi-level homes with walkout basements need enterprise-grade Wi-Fi solutions to maintain consistent coverage for security cameras, smart locks, and streaming devices.

Plymouth's newer developments along Vicksburg Lane and near the Elm Creek area offer better baseline wiring, but even recent construction may lack the Cat6 cabling and dedicated electrical circuits needed for a robust automation system. Pre-wire consultation during construction saves significant cost compared to retrofit work.
