## Smart Home Challenges Unique to Minnetonka

Minnetonka's housing stock spans more than six decades of construction. Homes in the Minnetonka Hills and Glen Lake neighborhoods, many built between 1955 and 1975, often feature older electrical panels (100-amp service), limited low-voltage wiring, and plaster-over-lath walls that complicate wireless signal distribution. A professional installer can assess these constraints and design solutions like mesh networking, structured wiring retrofits, or powerline adapters.

The city's rolling terrain and dense tree cover create Wi-Fi dead zones on larger properties. Lakefront homes in particular may struggle with connectivity in lower levels, boathouses, and detached garages. Our installers address this with enterprise-grade wireless access points and outdoor-rated network equipment.

New construction in developments near Carlson Towers and along Minnetonka Boulevard offers the advantage of pre-wiring, but builders don't always install the right cabling for full automation. Working with a smart home installer during the build phase ensures proper placement of Cat6 runs, speaker wire, and conduit for future expansion.
