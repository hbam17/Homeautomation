## Smart Home Challenges Unique to Hopkins

Hopkins has some of the oldest housing stock in the west metro. Cottageville homes from the 1920s and 1930s may have knob-and-tube wiring remnants, plaster walls with irregular stud spacing, and very small electrical panels. These homes need an electrician's assessment before advanced automation. However, wireless products work beautifully in these settings — Lutron Caseta switches install without neutral wires, and battery-powered cameras bypass wiring entirely.

The city's mix of single-family homes, duplexes, and multi-unit buildings along Excelsior Boulevard creates varied installation contexts. Condo and townhome residents have additional considerations: HOA rules for exterior modifications, shared-wall soundproofing for audio systems, and Wi-Fi interference from neighboring units. Our installers navigate these constraints routinely.

Hopkins' tree-lined streets and mature landscaping are community treasures that occasionally challenge outdoor camera placement and Wi-Fi signal reach to detached garages. Purpose-built outdoor access points and strategic camera mounting solve these issues without affecting aesthetics.
